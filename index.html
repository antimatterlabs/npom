Rooted in Legacy.
Proud of Home.

Established in the late 1700s, North Preston is Canada's oldest and largest land-settled Black community. It is a place of deep faith, family heritage, resilience, and pride. For generations, families have built lives here, supported by neighborhood ties, church connections, and a proud history of self-determination.

Today, as housing costs rise and local opportunities shift, our community faces the risk of displacement. Younger generations and seniors alike struggle to find affordable, suitable homes. NPOM is working to ensure that our people do not have to leave their home to find a home. We build to keep North Preston together.

Led by the Community.

North Preston Outreach Ministry (NPOM) is governed by a dedicated board of community members, professionals, and elders committed to delivering excellence, honesty, and community-first development.

By working in partnership with community organizations, municipal, provincial, and federal representatives, we ensure that our governance models and project specifications meet the highest standards of accountability.

All three levels of government coming together to support this initiative.

Visible.
Approachable.
Accountable.

NPOM is committed to earning community trust through absolute operational transparency, clear communication, and financial stewardship.

Residents will be invited into the conversation through outreach, public engagement, community events, and regular updates — long before any ground is broken. The shape of this project should reflect the people it serves.

Our strict bylaws guarantee that no board member or director can profit from NPOM's projects or assets. Every dollar goes directly into community housing. The board also requires local resident representation to ensure decisions reflect the community's interests.

100% of financial contributions are allocated directly to planning, architectural drawings, community events, and site preparation. No board members or directors receive salaries or private compensation.

Funds are managed by the NPOM Treasurer under strict oversight by the Board of Directors. All transactions are logged, and financial reports are presented annually at our Annual General Meeting (AGM).

We issue official charitable tax receipts for all eligible donations through our registered non-profit partnerships. Safe, secure banking details can be provided upon request.

All board decisions are subject to strict conflict-of-interest policies, and meeting notes, milestones, and annual reports are presented at our AGM and are always documented and available to residents upon request.
